What are Rect Construction Helpers?

In Flutter‚Äôs dart:ui, a Rect (rectangle) can be created in several ways. Each constructor is useful depending on what you already know: top-left + size, four edges, center + size, circle bounds, or two opposite corners.

Below are the five main Rect construction helpers. Each is already explained in detail (with code and screenshots) on the What is Rect? page. Here you get a short summary and a direct link to that section so you know what‚Äôs going on and where to read more.

Constructor overview

Rect.fromLTWH ‚Äî From left, top, width, height

Build a rect when you know the top-left corner (left, top) and the width and height. Very common when positioning from an origin point and a size.

Rect.fromLTRB ‚Äî From left, top, right, bottom

Rect.fromCenter ‚Äî From center point and size

Build a rect from the center point and the width and height. The rect is centered at that point. Useful when you position by center (e.g. canvas center).

Rect.fromCircle ‚Äî From center and radius

Build the bounding box of a circle: you pass the circle‚Äôs center and radius. The resulting rect is the axis-aligned square that contains the circle. Often used with drawArc or drawOval.

Rect.fromPoints ‚Äî From two corner points

Build a rect from any two opposite corners (e.g. top-left and bottom-right, or top-right and bottom-left). The rect is the smallest axis-aligned rectangle that contains both points.
